Tours & Activities

Peterborough Cathedral

One of the city's most iconic landmarks, Peterborough Cathedral is a magnificent medieval cathedral with a rich history. Its stunning architecture, including the famous West Front and Gothic interiors, attracts visitors from all over the world. The cathedral also houses the tomb of Katherine of Aragon, the first wife of King Henry VIII.

Nene Park

Situated on the banks of the River Nene, Nene Park is a vast green space covering around 1,725 acres. It offers a range of outdoor activities, including walking trails, cycling routes, water sports, and wildlife spotting. The park is home to Ferry Meadows Country Park, where you can enjoy picturesque lakes, woodlands, and meadows.

Peterborough Museum

Housed in an impressive Victorian mansion, the Peterborough Museum showcases the history and heritage of the city. The museum's collections include archaeology, fine art, and social history exhibits. Of particular interest is the reconstructed Victorian operating theater, which provides a glimpse into medical practices of the past.

Flag Fen Archaeology Park

This archaeological site dates back over 3,500 years and provides fascinating insights into Bronze Age life. Visitors can explore the reconstructed Bronze Age village, walk along the raised wooden causeway, and learn about the ancient artifacts discovered at the site. Flag Fen offers guided tours and interactive exhibits to enhance the visitor experience.

Railworld Wildlife Haven

A unique attraction for both train enthusiasts and nature lovers, Railworld combines a railway museum with a wildlife conservation area. Visitors can admire vintage locomotives and model railways while also exploring the nature reserve, which features ponds, meadows, and bird hides.
